Biography

Corliss Williamson was born in Arkansas on December 4, 1973. Power forward nicknamed Big Nasty; he played for five NBA teams from 1995 to 2007, winning an NBA Championship in 2004 with the Detroit Pistons; named the NBA’s 6th Man of the Year with the Pistons in 2002. During one stretch with the Kings, he formed a dominant frontcourt alongside Chris Webber. He was a high school McDonald’s All-American in 1992, before attending the University of Arkansas. Under legendary coach Nolan Richardson, Williamson led Arkansas to the 1994 NCAA Championship and took Most Outstanding Player honors.
